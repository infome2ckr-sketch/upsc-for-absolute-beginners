Step Zero Recap

Understand the game in one minute

UPSC has three rounds. Prelims in May or June has two papers but only GS Paper 1 counts for cutoff. Over ten lakh apply, about thirteen to fourteen thousand pass. Its only job is to screen people out. Mains in September is nine papers, seven count for rank. This is five days of writing answers, and this decides your rank. Interview in March or April is a thirty minute conversation worth 275 marks. They test personality and thinking, not memory.

Your Starter Kit

Six things, not fifty

Coaching will sell you a library. You need six sources. Cost about 2,500 rupees, less second-hand.

01

UPSC Syllabus PDF

Your GPS. Print it. Stick above your table. Read 2 minutes daily. Every question comes from here.

02

Last 10 Years PYQs

Do not solve yet. Read one Prelims paper today. Notice the shift to how many pairs are correct format.

03

Polity: M Laxmikanth 7th Ed

Covers about 15 percent Prelims and most of Mains GS2. Start Chapter 1 Historical Background.

04

History: NCERT + Spectrum

Class 12 Themes in Indian History Parts 1, 2, 3 plus Spectrum Brief History. Start 10 pages a day from Themes Part 1.

05

Geography: NCERT 11 + GC Leong

Fundamentals of Physical Geography Class 11 and GC Leong. Begin with Class 11 Chapter 2.

06

Current Affairs

Pick The Hindu or Indian Express plus one monthly compilation from Vision or Insights. Today: front page and one editorial.

Do not buy optional books yet. Depth in these six beats touching twenty sources once.

FOUNDATION MONTH

Your first 30 days

Goal is not to finish syllabus. Goal is to kill fear and build habit.

The 4-hour daily routine

Works for college or job

HOUR 1

Newspaper • 60 min

Read only three things: syllabus-linked news like Supreme Court judgments, one editorial for opinion, one international issue. Make maximum five bullet points.

HOUR 2-3

Static Subject • 120 min

Weeks 1 and 2: Polity Laxmikanth Chapters 1 to 10, about 8 pages a day, read each page twice. Weeks 3 and 4: History Class 12 Themes, 10 pages a day. After every chapter, close book and write five points you remember. That is active recall.

HOUR 4

Revise + PYQ • 60 min

First 30 minutes: revise what you studied yesterday. Memory is built by revisiting. Next 30 minutes: open PYQ PDF, find two questions from your chapter, try them. You will get them wrong. Perfect. Now you know the depth UPSC wants.

Weekends

Give one sectional Prelims test on Polity or History from any free site. No pressure, just pattern recognition.

Zero Days Policy

Even if sick or traveling, read one page. Keep the chain unbroken. Consistency beats intensity.
