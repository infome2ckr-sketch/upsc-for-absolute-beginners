Monetary Policy – UPSC Simplified

Not coaching notes. Just what UPSC asks.

•

Monetary Policy is the policy through which the Reserve Bank of India controls three things: how much money is in the economy, the cost of that money (interest rates), and how easily banks can provide credit to the public. RBI handles this, not the government; government manages Fiscal Policy through budgets and taxes.

Five Core Objectives

🎯

1. Price Stability

primary mandate under inflation targeting

📈

2. Economic Growth

supporting investment and production

🛡️

3. Financial Stability

prevent market crashes

💧

4. Liquidity Management

handling day-to-day cash

💱

5. Exchange Rate Stability

smoothing excessive Rupee volatility, not fixing rate

Remember this line for Mains: Monetary Policy is RBI's tool to control inflation and support growth, but price stability legally comes first.

Repo Rate: rate RBI lends short-term to banks against govt securities. Up = costly loans, inflation cools. Down = boost growth. Current: 5.25% (April 2026), stance Neutral.

SDF (floor): banks park excess without collateral. Current: 5.00% (25 bps below Repo)

MSF (ceiling): emergency overnight borrowing. Current: 5.50% (25 bps above Repo)

Quantity Tools

CRR – 4.5%

% deposits banks keep as cash with RBI, no interest paid. Current: 4.5%

SLR – 18%

18% of deposits in safe assets like govt bonds

OMO

RBI buying/selling bonds. Note: 2025 RBI used ₹1 lakh crore OMO purchase and $5 billion Rupee-swap

long-term, aligned with MSF at 5.50%

Reverse Repo

Decision Framework

Flexible Inflation Targeting

Since 2016: target 4% CPI ±2% (2% to 6% band)

MPC Structure

6 members – 3 from RBI (Governor as chair) + 3 external govt appointees. Each one vote, Governor casting vote on tie. Meets at least 4 times/year.

Mains Analysis – Trade-offs

cutting too fast spikes food inflation; high rates hurt jobs. Flexible targeting allows growth support within band.
Repo cut fails if banks don't pass to consumers. EBLR linking helped, but slowed by deposit costs and small savings competition.
2025-26 inflation hit 77-month low of 2.1% in June 2025, food inflation negative, giving RBI Goldilocks space. Governor Sanjay Malhotra (took office late 2024) emphasized neutral liquidity.
Rupee near 90 per Dollar in 2025, RBI used Dollar-Rupee swaps instead of aggressive hikes.

Current Affairs 2025-26

  • •Repo held at 5.25% with Neutral stance through 2026

  • •GDP Growth projected 6.5% to 7.3% for FY26
  • •Governor Malhotra stressed support amid US-Iran tensions and trade tariff risks
